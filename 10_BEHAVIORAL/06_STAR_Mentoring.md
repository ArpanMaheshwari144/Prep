# STAR — Mentored a New Joinee (codebase + DB + tools walkthrough)  [Konovo]

> Q ye cover karta: "teamwork batao" · "kisi ki help / mentor kiya" · "knowledge sharing" ·
> "bina bole aage aaya (proactive)" · "leadership without authority" · "junior guide kiya".
> (Resume line: "mentored a junior engineer and led PR reviews across the team.")

---

## STAR (Hinglish scaffold — yaad ke liye)

```
   S (Situation): team me ek NEW JOINEE aaya — capable tha, achha kaam kar raha tha, par ek part pe ATAK gaya.
                  wo part = jispe MAINE pehle DEEP kaam kiya tha (vendor portal / [apna module bharo]).
                  usko dikkat: codebase navigate karna, DB logic samajhna, internal tools use karna.

   T (Task): mujhe FORMALLY mentor assign nahi kiya tha. par us area ka deep context mere paas tha ->
             maine KHUD aage aaya usko unblock karne — usse atka chhodne ke bajaye.

   A (Action) — yehi meat hai (specific karo):
      - dedicated SCREEN-SHARE / meeting ki (sirf ek Slack reply nahi).
      - CODEBASE walk-through: "aise nahi -> ye pattern follow karo" (conventions dikhaye).
      - DB layer samjhaya: kaunsa table kya rakhta, data kaise flow karta, debug me kaise trace/query karo.
      - internal TOOLS dikhaye: kaunsa tool kis kaam ka, investigate kaise karte.
      - PR REVIEWS me follow-up: guiding feedback diya ("kyun" samjhaya) — sirf approve/reject nahi.

   R (Result): walkthrough ke baad wo KHUD kaafi problems solve karne laga -> us area me SELF-SUFFICIENT ho gaya,
               unblock, independently contribute karne laga. ramp-up time kam hua, team ko baar-baar hand-hold nahi karna pada.

   LESSON: mentoring = sirf jawab dena nahi -> MENTAL MODEL transfer karna (codebase + data + tools)
           taaki wo AGLA problem KHUD solve kar sake. (yehi "teach to fish" wali baat.)
```

---

## INTERVIEW DELIVERY (English, ~60-90 sec — bol-ke practice)

> **S:** "A new engineer had joined our team — sharp, ramping up well, but they got stuck on a part of the
> platform I had built and worked on deeply earlier — the vendor-facing side. They were struggling to
> navigate the codebase, understand the database flow, and use our internal tools."
>
> **T:** "I wasn't formally their mentor, but since I had the deepest context on that area, I decided to
> step up and unblock them rather than let them stay stuck."
>
> **A:** "I set up a proper screen-share session instead of a quick chat. I walked them through the codebase
> and the conventions we follow, explained the database layer — which tables held what and how the data
> flowed when debugging — and showed them how to use our internal tools to investigate. I also followed up
> through PR reviews, giving feedback that explained the *why*, not just approving or rejecting."
>
> **R:** "After that, they were able to solve most of those problems on their own — they became
> self-sufficient in that area and started contributing independently, which cut down their ramp-up time
> and freed the team from repeated hand-holding."
>
> **Lesson (agar poochein):** "It taught me mentoring isn't about answering the immediate question — it's
> about transferring the mental model so they can solve the *next* problem themselves."
